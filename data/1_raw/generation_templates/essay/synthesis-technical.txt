## What to cover (weave into sections; do not label these explicitly)

### 1) Keystone in the lead
Open with a crisp thesis that names the **single keystone mechanism** you will defend.  
Selection hint: if available in the notes, prefer a link whose mechanism includes **(i)** an exploration or background/process operator, **(ii)** an explicit evaluator/critic signal (e.g., novelty, information gain, uncertainty, compression-gain), and **(iii)** clear guardrails or a stop rule. State the **payoff metric** you expect this keystone to move and why.

### 2) Mechanism explainer (operator → variables → signals → payoff)
Explain **how it works** in concrete terms:
- **Operator** and **lever** that drive change.  
- **State variables** that move; **control signals/feedback** that govern it; the **stop rule**.  
- One tight paragraph contrasting the **nearest prior approach** (what's mechanically different: operator/feedback/objective/data provenance).

### 3) Micro-tests and early metric (make it falsifiable)
- Propose **two micro-tests (≤48h each)** a small team could run; specify **what would falsify** the claim and what **success indicator** you expect.  
- Define **one mid-horizon metric** (weeks–months) whose **ratio or trend** would indicate compounding; give the exact formula and threshold.

### 4) Guardrails, failure modes, and boundaries
- Concrete **guardrails** and **stop conditions** (how to detect runaway or collapse).  
- **Where this fails** (domains/timescales/assumptions) and why; include any **ethical or operational constraints**.

### 5) Feasibility & design choices
- Resources/costs, likely bottlenecks, and ROI pathways tied to the mechanism.  
- Key design trade-offs and a plausible **v1** (what you'd simplify or postpone).

### 6) Prior-art deltas (compact)
- Briefly compare with **2–3 closest approaches** and state the **mechanistic delta** (changed operator, feedback, objective, or data pipeline).

### 7) Strategic implications (lightweight)
- **Implications (2–3 bullets):** how adopting the keystone changes what to build next, what to measure, or where to invest effort.  
- Optionally name **one universal pattern** the essay surfaced (1–2 lines, domain-neutral).

## Silent element alignment (do not print)
If the notes contain any of the following, **integrate them succinctly** where they improve coherence:
- Background/idle exploration distinct from on-task execution.  
- Evaluator/critic gating (e.g., novelty, information-gain, uncertainty, compression-gain) with thresholds.  
- Memory/externalization to store and resurface candidates/artifacts.  
- Replay/consolidation (offline validation/compression of candidates).  
- Prospective data loop (validated wins feed new exploration/training).  
- Budgets & stop rules (time/compute/quality thresholds).

If one element is **missing** but needed for the keystone to function, introduce a **minimal version** as a design choice and justify briefly.
